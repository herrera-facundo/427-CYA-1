# Reflection

## Activity Description

For this career development experience, I participated in a startup pitch competition related to a software application being developed as part of my senior capstone project. The application is being created for an external client, and the competition provided an opportunity to present the project to judges, investors, and other participants.

The primary objective of the event was to gather feedback on the viability of the project and explore potential funding opportunities that could allow the project to continue beyond the academic setting. The event lasted two days and included tabling sessions, formal presentations, discussions with judges, and networking with other participants.

During the preparation process, my team and I worked on organizing the presentation, refining how the project was communicated, and ensuring that the key aspects of the application were clearly explained. During the competition itself, I helped present the technical components of the system and participated in discussions with attendees about the potential applications and future development of the project.

## Technical or Professional Decisions

One of the most important decisions during the preparation process was determining how to most effectively balance the technical and business aspects of the system to a mixed audience. While the application includes complex technical elements, the audience included individuals without a technical background. As a result, we focused on clearly explaining the problem being addressed and the value of the solution rather than emphasizing low-level implementation details.

Another important decision involved structuring the presentation to fit the limited time available during the pitch competition. We needed to prioritize the most impactful aspects of the project while still providing enough technical context to demonstrate that the system was feasible and well designed.

## Contributions

This experience involved collaboration with my capstone team. My individual contributions included helping prepare the pitch materials, explaining the technical architecture of the system during the presentation, and participating in question-and-answer discussions with judges and attendees. I also contributed to refining how the technical components of the application were described in the presentation.

## Quality Assessment

Overall, I believe my performance during the event was effective. I was able to clearly explain the technical aspects of the project and respond to questions from judges and attendees. The experience provided valuable practice in presenting technical work in a professional environment.

Additionally, we managed to secure the interest of an investor who is willing to help materialize the project. This can open up post-graduation employment opportunities.

If I were to repeat this experience, I would spend more time practicing the presentation and refining the balance between technical detail and accessibility for non-technical audiences. Improving this balance would make the presentation even more effective for a broader audience.

## Career Alignment

This experience directly supported my career development by exposing me to the intersection of software engineering and entrepreneurship. Presenting a technical project to investors required combining technical understanding with communication and strategic thinking.

The competition also reinforced the possibility of continuing the project beyond the academic setting, which aligns with my long-term interest in contributing to impactful technology solutions and potentially participating in startup ventures.
